Projects

Some of the shenanigans I get up to

Sphero Swarm

My friends and I are working on using a ton of Sphero Bolts to simulate chemical reactions! The basic idea is to have these Spheros moving 'randomly' around a confined space, and when they collide, they 'bond' together to form molecules. It's a fun way to visualize how atoms interact and form compounds. We're pretty close to getting it all working, and once we do, we're planning to display it at Imagination Station (a science center in Lafayette). Hopefully, it'll get some kids interested in CS and chemistry!

Mash

Mash is the name I gave to the shell interpreter I built for my Systems Programming class. It's fully functional, memory-safe command-line interface that allows users to just about anything they can do with Bash. I've implemented features like piping, background execution, built-in commands like 'cd' and 'exit', wildcarding, running subshells, command piping, io redirection, process substitution, environment variable expansion/support, and line editing.

ChemECar

I'm the Mechanical Lead for Purdue's ChemECar team! Every year, we build a small car powered by 2 chemical reactions: 1 to propel the car and 1 to figure out when the car should stop. As the Mechanical Lead, I not only program the Arduino microcontroller to figure out when the car should stop, but my team also designs and make the car! This has given me a lot of experience with CAD software, specifically Fusion 360. Since we build a new car every year, I've also gotten to work with some really, really cool manufacturing tools (ex. Last semester, I got the opportunity to print something on the Formlabs Fuse 1. I've always been a 3d printing nerd, so getting the chance to use an SLS machine was a dream come true)
